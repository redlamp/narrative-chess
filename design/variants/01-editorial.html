Two players sit down. A board opens. Somewhere between the first move and the last, a story arrives — and we publish it.

Begin a game Sign in

The game is older than every word written about it, and yet each game is unrepeatable. Players carry a tone — bold, hesitant, lyrical, brutal — into a finite grid, and what comes out the other side is, in our reading, a piece of literature.

This site is an effort to take that reading seriously. Every game is a document. Every move is a sentence. The pieces walk where they must, and we are merely the ones who read what they have written.

The bishop and the rook: a quiet correspondence.

By the editors  ·  A study in long diagonals

What the bishop says, the rook eventually answers. We followed a correspondence game across nine days and found, in the lulls between moves, the same patient music we hear in epistolary fiction.

This is the slot, in M2, where the narrative layer surfaces. Cities. Characters. Story beats threaded through a real game's score.
